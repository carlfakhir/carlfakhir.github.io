§01 / about

A short biography, mostly true.

I’m a CS student at Georgia Tech who likes building products with a strong technical core: systems that have to reason, visualize, predict, or make complicated data easier to use. The fun for me sits somewhere between an honest research prototype and a piece of software you actually want to keep open.

Day to day that has looked like cloud cost attribution, browser-side ML, and local-first software. I like projects where the underlying idea is a little weird but the product still feels crisp and practical.

I’m especially drawn to applied ML, AI infrastructure, developer tools, and the part of a product where research becomes opinion. Opinionated beats configurable almost every time.

    Pairs Trading Backtester

    Statistical-arbitrage research stack. Screens a 60+ ticker universe for cointegrated pairs (Engle–Granger + ADF + OU half-life), backtests a z-score mean-reversion strategy on a held-out window with realistic transaction costs, and reports per-pair and portfolio Sharpe, max drawdown, and hit rate. Train and test windows are walk-forward, never overlapping.
